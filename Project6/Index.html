    Common Symptoms Include

    • Fever or chills
    • Cough
    • Fatigue
    • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
    • Headache
    • New loss of taste or smell
    • Sore throat
    • Congestion or runny nose

    Precaution From Covid-19

      Wear a Mask: Wear a mask that covers your nose and mouth when in public settings, especially when it's difficult to maintain social distancing.

      Practice Social Distancing: Maintain a distance of at least 6 feet (about 2 meters) from others who are not from your household, especially in crowded areas.

      Wash Your Hands: Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in public places, touching surfaces frequently touched by others, or after coughing, sneezing, or blowing your nose. If soap and water are not available, use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

      Avoid Close Contact: Avoid close contact with people who are sick. Stay home as much as possible, especially if you are feeling unwell.

      Cover Your Mouth and Nose: Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when you cough or sneeze, and then dispose of the used tissue properly.

      Follow Public Health Guidelines: Stay informed about the latest recommendations from public health authorities and follow their guidance.

    #FAQs - Frequently asked questions

    Q: What are the symptoms of the coronavirus disease?

    A: The most common symptoms are fever, cough, shortness of breath, and breathing difficulties. In more severe cases infection can cause pneumonia, severe acute respiratory syndrome, and even death. The period within which the symptoms would appear is 2-14 days.

    Q: What is the recovery time for the coronavirus disease?

    A: Using available preliminary data, the median time from onset to clinical recovery for mild cases is approximately 2 weeks and is 3-6 weeks for patients with severe or critical disease.

    Q: How long does the coronavirus last on surfaces?

    A: It is not certain how long the virus that causes COVID-19 survives on surfaces, but it seems to behave like other coronaviruses. Studies suggest that coronaviruses (including preliminary information on the COVID-19 virus) may persist on surfaces for a few hours or up to several days.

    Q: Who is most at risk of severe illness from COVID-19?

    A: Older adults and individuals with underlying health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, lung disease, or weakened immune systems are at higher risk of developing severe illness or complications from COVID-19.

    Q: How can I protect myself and others from COVID-19?

    A: Practice good hygiene by washing your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, or using hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol. Wear a mask in public settings, maintain physical distance from others, avoid large gatherings, and stay home if you are feeling unwell.

    Q: Is there a vaccine for COVID-19?

    A: As of [2020], several COVID-19 vaccines were in development and clinical trials. However, none had received full approval for public distribution at that time. Efforts to develop and distribute vaccines were ongoing.

    Q: What should I do if I think I have COVID-19?

    A: If you develop symptoms of COVID-19 or have been in close contact with someone who has tested positive, contact your healthcare provider for guidance. Follow their instructions for testing, self-isolation, and monitoring your symptoms.
